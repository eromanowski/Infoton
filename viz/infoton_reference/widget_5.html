II · The Specification
The Byte Was a Market Choice, Not a Physical One
When the bit was given to IBM to structure, IBM designed the byte. The word was coined by Werner Buchholz in 1956 during the design of the Stretch machine, and its width was left open: his own memo defined a byte as any number of parallel bits from one to six. Across IBM and its competitors the byte was whatever each machine needed it to be. In 1961 Landauer established that information costs energy and produces heat. Three years later the byte was fixed at eight bits by System/360, with the governing equation absent from the sheet. Encoding updates have been done before. Converting to a new encoding is not theoretical, it is standard product deployment.
The Floor That Was Left Out
E ≥ kB T ln(2)
Boltzmann constant times temperature times the natural logarithm of two: the minimum energy any irreversible operation must dissipate as heat. Landauer published this in 1961, in IBM's own journal. Three years later the byte was fixed at eight bits with this term absent, leaving computation with unbound energy parameters. The ability to redesign the byte against this floor has existed since the 1950s.
Encoding Updates Have Been Done Before
Precedent · 1993
The encoding has already been replaced once.
In 1993, Unicode replaced ASCII as the character encoding in a shipping operating system, Windows NT 3.1, and ISO/IEC 10646 was published. The whole industry, IBM, Microsoft, Apple, Sun, Novell, NeXT, agreed to move off the fixed 8-bit scheme. Converting to a new encoding is not theoretical, it is standard product deployment.
Deployable Now · 2026
P30 can start deploying immediately.
The same kind of encoding update, done as standard product deployment, can begin now to address the water, energy, and heat crisis the 8-bit byte's checking overhead drives in data centers.
