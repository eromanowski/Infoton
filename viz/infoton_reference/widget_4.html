P30 at Population Scale: Reactors Never Built
Each data center converted from the byte to the Infoton drops its compute energy by the benchmarked 25.08×. The small remainder is served by solar and wind, so the nuclear reactors proposed to meet this demand are not reduced, they are all avoided. At full conversion the reactors needed fall to zero. Drag the deployment slider to convert the fleet and watch every proposed reactor turn green.

The Math, Step by Step
1
US fleet: 183 TWh/yr ÷ 5,500 DCs = 3.80 MW avg Average per-data-center draw, derived from real US consumption.
2
Energy ratio (benchmark) = 41.078 / 1.638 = 25.08× Byte vs Infoton per message, at the Landauer floor.
3
Converted DC = 3.80 / 25.08 = 0.15 MW Each conversion removes 3.65 MW, 96% of that center's draw.
4
Fleet(d) = n × [(1−d)·3.80 + d·0.15] d = fraction converted. The slider sets d.
5
Saved(d) = n × d × 3.65 MW Scales linearly with deployment.
6
SMRs avoided = Saved ÷ 300 MW Full global conversion: 43 GW = 143 SMRs never built.

Fleet demand: P(d) = n × [(1−d) × 3.80 + d × 0.15] MW, where n is fleet size and d the converted fraction. Demand eliminated = n × d × 3.65 MW. SMRs avoided = that ÷ 300 MW. The 25.08× ratio is benchmark-exact; per-DC average, reactor size, and PUE are stated estimates.
US fleet ~5,500 data centers consuming ~183 TWh in 2024; global fleet ~11,800 (IEA, Statista, Brightlio, 2025–2026). Average draw is fleet energy divided by count and understates flagship AI campuses while overstating small edge sites; the ratio holds regardless. This visual argues efficiency removes the demand driving the reactor pipeline; it does not endorse nuclear or any extractive baseload. Non-extractive nameplate uses standard U.S. capacity factors, paired with storage and demand shaping.
