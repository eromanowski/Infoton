The Cost of a 64-Bit Word
Information is a state of matter [1][2], and because it is a state of matter it has density [3], viscosity, energy transfer, energy conservation, and is subject to manipulation and mutation [4]. Any system that wants to guarantee the bits it reads match the bits it wrote must continuously spend computation detecting and correcting the drift caused by heat, leakage, radiation, and noise [5]. These checks cost computation, computation costs energy [6][7], and that energy is dissipated as heat and entropy [8], the very disturbances the checking exists to fight.
Error correction cannot be paid for one byte at a time. A single flipped bit anywhere in the word forces the machine to read and recompute across all sixty-four data bits, all eight bytes, because the redundancy that locates the error is spread across the entire word, not stored per byte. This is that tax, made visible: every operation the machine performs simply to verify the data did not change.

A ninth byte of pure overhead rides with every eight. The 8 check bits follow a Hsiao (72,64) matrix [9][10], each covering 26 of the 64 data bits. This 12.5% is the floor for one ECC scheme; real facilities layer replication or erasure coding on top [11]. It is a storage tax, separate from the per-access energy cost.
