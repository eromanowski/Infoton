Infoton P30
The Infoton P30 update leverages cryptographic number theory, where the natural periodicity in the numbers coprime to 30 has been documented since 1993 in the prime spiral structure [12], with the underlying mathematics shown in 2020 in The Prime state and its quantum relatives [13]. By following cryptographic number theory, faster computation is enabled: the natural pauses in the numbers provide the error-correction check directly. Twenty-two of every thirty values are divisible by 2, 3, or 5, so 73.3% of the number line is structurally excluded, and a value either lands on one of the eight coprime spokes, where it is valid by construction, or it does not, where it is caught on sight. The integrity check is the structure itself, so no separate redundancy pass is owed. The lower the demand on energy, the less heat produced, which puts less wear on computation parts, and cascades outward into less water used for cooling, reduced demand for nuclear power, and less pressure to scale data centers.

At short lengths P30 is not about making files smaller; on this sentence it is within a few bytes of UTF-8. What it buys instead is built-in integrity: the data carries its own correctness, so no separate error-correction layer has to be stored or maintained alongside it. The size advantage arrives on longer data; the integrity advantage is there from the first character.

Only the lab experiment sits near the floor, and only because it switches adiabatically slowly. Of buyable memory, MRAM is roughly fifty times leaner than DRAM because it holds data with no refresh, yet both remain far above the thermodynamic minimum. Lowest energy comes from doing fewer operations, which is what P30 removes, not from reaching the floor.
