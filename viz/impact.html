Infoton P30 Computational Efficiencies Eliminate the Need for Water Cooling
Byte-based data centers as they are designed today leverage water resources because the byte performs so many computations checking for parity in the message sent and received that, to keep the heat under control, additional cooling methods are required. The Infoton, because it performs low computations and follows cryptographic number theory, produces low amounts of heat and entropy. One needs water. One does not.

The Math, Step by Step
1
Operation ratio = 12,264 / 489 = 25.1× Conservative library profile: P30 re-verifies every read (Locate + Emit + Verify), like-for-like with Hamming's read-side check. Native BIOS-native silicon (reads-free) reaches 163 ops → 75.2×, conditional on the coprime-integrity claim (pending BER validation).
2
Power scales linearly: P = N × ops × Eop Same throughput N, same silicon Eop: only the op-count differs.
3
P30 rack power = 130 kW × (489 / 12,264) = 5.18 kW The byte's 130 kW load, recomputed at P30's library op-count. (Native reads-free profile: 1.73 kW.)
4
No-water: 5.18 kW ≤ 30 kW air ceiling  ✓ Byte: 130 kW > 30 kW → water required. P30: 5.18 kW < 30 kW → air only.
5
Threshold: N* = Qair / (ops × Eop) rises 25.1× P30 tolerates 25.1× the throughput before any water is ever needed (up to 75.2× on native reads-free silicon).

No-water condition: P(N) = N × ops × Eop ≤ Qair. Threshold: N* = Qair / (ops × Eop). Cutting ops from 12,264 to 489 raises N* by 25.1× (to 163 ops → 75.2× on native reads-free silicon).
Air-cooling ceiling 20–35 kW per rack, GB200 NVL72 rack 120–132 kW, evaporative draw 2–5 million gallons per MW per year: 2025–2026 industry figures. Two P30 profiles: the library profile (489 ops, re-verify every read) gives the conservative 25.1× shown here and in the interactive demo; the BIOS-native profile (163 ops, reads trusted without re-verification) gives 75.2× but is contingent on the coprime-integrity claim, pending the dual-FPGA bit-error-rate validation. Operation ratios are exact and substrate-independent; absolute kW and water figures depend on the stated cooling and per-operation estimates, and scale only the validation portion of the load. Total facility power includes compute, memory, and networking beyond validation.
